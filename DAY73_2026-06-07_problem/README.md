 # Product Cannibalization Analysis After New Product Launch

## Problem Statement

A company regularly launches new products to expand its portfolio and capture additional market share. However, a newly launched product may unintentionally reduce the sales of an existing product that serves a similar customer segment. This phenomenon is known as **Product Cannibalization**.

Your task is to analyze sales performance before and after each product launch and determine whether the newly launched product negatively impacted the sales of the existing product.

The analysis should compare the performance of the impacted product during a 30-day period before the launch and a 30-day period after the launch.

The objective is to identify severe, moderate, or negligible cannibalization effects and highlight products that experienced growth after the launch of a new product.

---

## Business Objective

The business wants to answer the following questions:

* Did the launch of a new product reduce the sales of an existing product?
* How significant was the decline in units sold?
* How significant was the decline in revenue?
* Which products experienced growth despite the launch?
* Which launches do not have enough historical data for comparison?

---

## Rules and Assumptions

### Rule 1: Pre-Launch Window

Consider sales of the impacted product occurring within the 30 days before the launch date.

Example:

Launch Date:

2024-02-01

Pre-launch period:

2024-01-02 to 2024-01-31

Only sales of the impacted product should be included.

---

### Rule 2: Post-Launch Window

Consider sales of the impacted product occurring within the 30 days after the launch date, including the launch date itself.

Example:

Launch Date:

2024-02-01

Post-launch period:

2024-02-01 to 2024-03-01

Only sales of the impacted product should be included.

---

### Rule 3: Ignore New Product Sales

Sales of the newly launched product must never be included in any calculation.

The objective is to measure the impact on the old product, not the performance of the new product.

Example:

If Product B is launched and Product A is the impacted product:

* Use Product A sales before launch
* Use Product A sales after launch
* Ignore Product B sales entirely

---

### Rule 4: Pre-Launch Units

Pre-launch units represent the total number of units sold by the impacted product during the pre-launch period.

---

### Rule 5: Post-Launch Units

Post-launch units represent the total number of units sold by the impacted product during the post-launch period.

---

### Rule 6: Unit Change Percentage

Calculate the percentage increase or decrease in units sold after launch.

Formula:

(Post Launch Units - Pre Launch Units)
/
Pre Launch Units
× 100

Positive values indicate growth.

Negative values indicate decline.

---

### Rule 7: Pre-Launch Revenue

Pre-launch revenue is the total revenue generated by the impacted product during the pre-launch period.

---

### Rule 8: Post-Launch Revenue

Post-launch revenue is the total revenue generated by the impacted product during the post-launch period.

---

### Rule 9: Revenue Change Percentage

Calculate the percentage increase or decrease in revenue after launch.

Formula:

(Post Launch Revenue - Pre Launch Revenue)
/
Pre Launch Revenue
× 100

Positive values indicate revenue growth.

Negative values indicate revenue decline.

---

### Rule 10: Handling Missing Historical Data

If pre-launch units are zero, percentage calculations cannot be performed.

In such cases:

* Unit Change Percentage = NULL
* Revenue Change Percentage = NULL
* Status = INSUFFICIENT_PRE_DATA

---

## Cannibalization Status Rules

### INSUFFICIENT_PRE_DATA

Assign this status when:

* Pre-launch units = 0

Reason:

There is no historical data available for comparison.

---

### SEVERE_CANNIBALIZATION

Assign this status when:

Unit Change Percentage ≤ -40%

Reason:

The product lost at least 40% of its sales after the launch of the new product.

---

### MODERATE_CANNIBALIZATION

Assign this status when:

Unit Change Percentage > -40%
AND
Unit Change Percentage ≤ -20%

Reason:

The product experienced a noticeable decline but not a severe collapse.

---

### GROWTH_AFTER_LAUNCH

Assign this status when:

Unit Change Percentage > 0

Reason:

The impacted product grew despite the launch of a competing product.

---

### NO_CANNIBALIZATION

Assign this status when:

Unit Change Percentage is between -20% and 0%

Reason:

The decline is too small to be considered meaningful cannibalization.

## Expected Output Columns

### launch_id

Unique launch event identifier.

### impacted_product_id

Existing product potentially affected by the new launch.

### new_product_id

Newly launched product.

### launch_date

Date when the new product was launched.

### pre_launch_units

Total units sold during the pre-launch period.

### post_launch_units

Total units sold during the post-launch period.

Percentage change in units sold after launch.

Total revenue generated during the pre-launch period.

Total revenue generated during the post-launch period.

Percentage change in revenue after launch.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Measure only the impacted product's performance.
* Ignore sales of the newly launched product.
* Compare a fixed 30-day period before and after launch.
* Analyze both units and revenue.
* Classify the impact using predefined business thresholds.
* Handle missing historical data separately.
* Focus on business impact rather than raw sales numbers.

This type of analysis is commonly used by Product Managers, Revenue Analysts, Category Managers, and Data Analysts to evaluate launch effectiveness and portfolio strategy.
